BRICOLAGE : Visualizing Tobacco Factories

D E S C R I P T I O N

"Bricolage:[mass noun] (in art or literature) construction or creation from a diverse range of available things:... [count noun] something constructed or created from a diverse range of things..."

Bricolage aggregates and visualizes indicative architectural features of tobacco factories. In four parts (top to bottom) the draft shows roof styles, facades, fencing, and finally exterior decoration (where it rarely occurs). The study reveals how thousands of tobacco factories retain local anonymity around the world.

The Cigarette Citadels project focuses on the tobacco industry with particular emphasis on the idea that it capitalizes on consumer addiction while evading culpability. The aptness of this particular study's focus is contingent upon the premise that there exists a societal predisposition to associate fault with a product rather than its producer. The current lack of visual recognition of cigarette factories thus is the main focus in exploring the materiality or face of tobacco.
