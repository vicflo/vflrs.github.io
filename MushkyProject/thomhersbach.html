About Me

I'm Thomas Hersbach, a 3rd year PhD Candidate in the Catalysis and Surface Chemistry (CASC) group at Leiden University.

I obtained my BSc in 2012 with distinction from Leiden University and Delft University of Technology (co-op bachelors program). My bachelor's thesis focused on electro-oxidation of polyols on gold & platinum, conducted in the (CASC) group.

In 2012 I began my masters at Leiden University. My thesis focused on spectro-electrochemical characterization of an iridium-based oxygen evolution catalyst. My final master's semester was spent at Stanford University, where I worked in the Jaramillo group to investigate the promoting effect of noble metals on the oxygen evolution activity of 3d metal oxides. I received my MSc with distinction in August 2014.

I am currently advised by physical electrochemist Mark Koper. My current thesis work focuses on nanoparticle formation by cathodic corrosion.

In my free time, I enjoy running, reading, home brewing, and making the world a better place; when it comes to chemistry I'm interested in:

  • Cathodic Corrosion
  • XAFS
  • Sustainability
